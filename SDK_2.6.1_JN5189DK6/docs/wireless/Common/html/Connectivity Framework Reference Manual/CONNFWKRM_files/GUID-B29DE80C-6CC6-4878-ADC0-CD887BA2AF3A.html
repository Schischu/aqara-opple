Full power down mode

Set macros cPWR_UsePowerDownMode and cPWR_FullPowerDownMode to 1 to enable the full power down modes. In full power down, the main digital domain, flash and most peripheral clocks and other clock sources are switched off. The ARM MCU and, if enabled, the Bluetooth LE controller are in deep sleep.
  • RAM may be switched off, or powered at a low-power retention level (in addition, the amount of retained RAM can be minimised by selection of specific application linker scripts)
  • The 32kHz clock may be switched off, or remain running for wake timers or the Bluetooth LE core
  • The always-on power domain (AO LDO) can be left at operating voltage or at a lower sleep voltage. Be aware the lower sleep voltage can affect the accuracy of the 32kHz FRO clock
The modes available are as follows:
Table 1. Modules available
Mode 32 kHZ clock RAM AO LDO level Possible wake sources
1 On On Build-defined Bluetooth LE core, wake timers, RTC, GPIO, SPI0, USART0, BODVBAT, NTAG
2 On Off Build-defined Bluetooth LE core, wake timers, RTC, GPIO, SPI0, USART0, BODVBAT, NTAG
3 On On Sleep level Bluetooth LE core, wake timers, RTC, GPIO, SPI0, USART0, BODVBAT, NTAG
5 Off On Sleep level RTC, GPIO, SPI0, USART0, BODVBAT, NTAG
6 Off Off Sleep level RTC, GPIO, SPI0, USART0, BODVBAT, NTAG
Note:
  • To set a mode, the mode number is passed as the parameter to PWR_ChangeDeepSleepMode
  • Build-defined AO LDO level is defined by the macros gClkUseFro32K and gPWR_UseAlgoTimeBaseDriftCompensate. If gClkUseFro32K=1 and gPWR_UseAlgoTimeBaseDriftCompensate=0, the AO LDO is left at operating voltage. Otherwise, sleep level voltage is used
